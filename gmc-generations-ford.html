What is Website Verification?

Google needs proof that you own your dealership website before it will trust you with features like Google Shopping, vehicle listings, and local inventory ads. Think of it like showing your ID at the dealership — it proves you're the real deal.

Good news: You only need to do this once. After verification, Google remembers you. You don't need to re-do it unless the tag is removed from your website.

Step 1: Get Your Verification Tag from Google Dealership

Dealership Action Required

The dealership (or someone with access to the Google Merchant Center account) must log in and retrieve the unique verification tag. This tag is specific to your dealership — no one else can use it.

Log into your Google Merchant Center account and navigate to Settings → Business Information → Website. Select the "HTML tag" method. Google will display a unique meta tag for your account.

Example Format — Your Tag Will Look Like This
name="google-site-verification" content="[GOOGLE_VERIFICATION_TOKEN_PLACEHOLDER]" />

Your tag is unique to your dealership. The placeholder above is for illustration only. Always use the exact tag Google generates inside your own Merchant Center account. Never share this tag publicly.

Step 2: Add the Tag in Dealer.com Web Agency / Dealer.com

Agency / Dealer.com Action Required

Your web agency or Dealer.com administrator must paste the verification tag into the Custom Head Code field in the CMS dashboard and publish the change.

  1. Log into Dealer.com / Dealertrack CMS
    Go to your Dealer.com Content Management System (CMS) — the dashboard where you manage your dealership website. You likely already use this to update photos, prices, and specials. If you're not sure how to log in, contact your Ford Digital Program representative or your agency.
  2. Navigate to "Site Settings" or "SEO" Section
    In your Dealer.com dashboard, look for a section called "Site Settings," "SEO," "Advanced Settings," or "Head Tag Management." This is typically found under a gear icon ⚙️ or in the main navigation menu. Tip: If you can't find it, search for "meta tags" or "head code" in the Dealer.com help search.
  3. Find the "Custom Head Code" or "Header Scripts" Field
    Dealer.com provides a dedicated text box where you can add custom HTML code that goes into the section of every page. This is exactly where Google wants the verification tag. It may be labeled:
    • "Custom Header Code"
    • " Tag Injection"
    • "Additional Meta Tags"
    • "SEO — Custom Header Scripts"
  4. Paste Your Verification Tag
    Paste the entire meta tag you copied from Google into the custom head code field. Do not add anything else, and do not modify the tag in any way. It should look exactly like what Google gave you.
  5. Save and Publish Your Changes
    Click "Save," "Apply," or "Publish." Dealer.com will push this change to your live website. This may take 5–15 minutes to fully propagate across the web. While you wait, you can move to the next step.

Dealer.com (Ford Digital Program) — Important Notes

If you're working through a Ford Digital Program (Dealertrack Tier 3), here's what you should know:

  • You don't need FTP or file access. Dealer.com's CMS handles everything through the dashboard — no technical file uploading required.
  • The tag goes in the of every page automatically. When you add it to the "Custom Head Code" field in Dealer.com, it applies site-wide. You only need to paste it once.
  • If your site is managed by an agency: Forward this guide to them. They should be able to complete this in under 5 minutes. Most Ford dealership agencies are already familiar with Dealer.com's interface.
  • Dealer.com support: If you can't locate the right setting, Dealer.com's support team can walk you through it. Reference: "Adding custom meta tags to the head section."
  • SSL / HTTPS: Your Ford Digital site should already be HTTPS. Google requires a secure site for verification. If your site shows a "Not Secure" warning, contact your Digital Program rep first.

Step 3: Confirm Tag is Live (Coordinator Action) Coordinator

Coordinator Action Required

Before clicking verify in Google Merchant Center, you must confirm the verification tag is actually present on the dealership's homepage. This prevents unnecessary failed attempts.

Open your dealership's homepage in a browser. Right-click on the page and select "View Page Source" (or press Ctrl+U / Cmd+U). Search for google-site-verification to confirm the tag is present.

  1. Open Dealership Homepage
    Navigate to the exact homepage URL that is registered in Google Merchant Center (include or exclude www as appropriate).
  2. View Page Source
    Right-click anywhere on the page and select "View Page Source" or press Ctrl+U (Windows) / Cmd+Option+U (Mac).
  3. Search for Verification Tag
    Press Ctrl+F (Windows) or Cmd+F (Mac) and search for google-site-verification. You should see the meta tag with your unique content value.

Step 4: Verify in Google Merchant Center Coordinator

Coordinator Action Required

Now that the tag is confirmed live, log into Google Merchant Center and click the verify button. This action is performed by the coordinator, not the dealership or agency.

Return to the Google Merchant Center verification page where you obtained the tag. Click the "Verify Your Online Store" button. Google will check your homepage for the verification tag.

Critical: Don't Remove the Tag

Once verified, leave the HTML tag on your website permanently. Removing it will cause you to lose verification.

  • Removing it will cause you to lose verification — and you'll have to start over
  • It does not give Google access to your website's backend, CMS, or any sensitive data
  • It does not slow down your website or affect performance

If you switch agencies or redesign your site: Make sure the new team knows to keep the Google verification tag in place. If they rebuild the site and forget to add it back, you'll lose verification and need to re-verify.

Alternative: Upload an HTML Verification File

If the HTML tag method doesn't work or you prefer an alternative approach, you can download a verification HTML file from Google and upload it to your website. This method works differently — instead of adding code to your existing pages, you place a special file on your server.

Dealer.com note: Dealer.com's standard CMS may not give you direct file upload access to your web server's root directory. If you can't upload files directly, the HTML tag method (above) is recommended as it works entirely within the Dealer.com dashboard. However, some Dealer.com configurations do support file uploads — check with your rep.

Follow These Steps

Use this method if you have direct access to your website's file system (via FTP, SFTP, or a file manager in your hosting panel).

  1. Download the Verification HTML File from Google
    In Google Merchant Center, click the download link for your verification file. It will be named something like:

    google10bad35e588a7878.html

    Save this file to your computer. Do not rename it, open it, or modify its contents. The filename is part of the verification.
  2. Upload the File to Your Website's Root Directory
    Upload the file to the root directory of your website — the same folder where your index.html or homepage file lives. The file needs to be accessible at:

    https://www.yourdealership.com/google10bad35e588a7878.html

    For Dealer.com sites, this typically requires FTP access or a file manager through your hosting panel.
  3. Confirm the File is Accessible
    Open a browser and navigate to the full URL of the uploaded file (as shown above). You should see a page with text confirming your Google verification. If you see a 404 error or "page not found," the file isn't in the right location.
  4. Click "Verify Your Online Store" in Merchant Center
    Go back to Google Merchant Center and click the verify button. Google will check for the file at the expected URL. If found, your site is verified.

Dealer.com (Ford Digital) — File Upload Considerations

Dealer.com's CMS is primarily designed for content management through their dashboard interface, not direct file system access. Here's what you should know:

  • FTP access: Some Ford Digital Program configurations provide FTP/SFTP access. Check with your Digital Program rep if this is available for your dealership.
  • Alternative: If FTP isn't available, the HTML tag method is strongly recommended as it works within Dealer.com's CMS without needing file access.
  • Agency-managed sites: If your dealership website is managed by an external agency, they may have FTP access and can upload the file for you. Simply send them the file and the instructions above.
  • Don't delete after verification: Even after Google confirms verification, you must keep this file on your server. Deleting it will cause you to lose verification.
